Proposed Process (Automated System)
Business Process Model and Notation (BPMN) is utilized to visually represent the step-by-step operations of the NutriCare health management system. From an Information Systems perspective, analyzing the workflow is essential in identifying operational bottlenecks within the traditional manual tracking of the Rural Health Unit. This approach provides a clear graphical view of the end-to-end flow of seven integrated process streamsranging from role-based authentication to automated health monitoring highlighting the efficiency improvements introduced by the proposed automated system compared to the manual process.
The system’s process architecture is orchestrated through an event-driven design that balances synchronous user interactions with asynchronous background processing. When a health worker initiates a Maternal or Child Nutrition record, the system immediately executes validation logic and triggers background listeners. These listeners manage the automated dispatch of SMS and Email notifications via communication APIs and generate real-time Slack alerts for high-priority cases, such as malnourished children or high-risk pregnancies. By offloading these computationally heavy tasks including DomPDF-based report generation and complex BMI Z-score calculations to an asynchronous job queue, the system ensures a highly responsive interface for RHU staff while maintaining a rigorous audit trail of all health interventions.
After health assessments are completed, the system utilizes the gathered data to update Graphical Trend Lines, providing a visual representation of a patient's progress over time. For administrative compliance, the system features an on-demand Report Generation tool. Using predefined templates and the Dompdf library, the system transforms raw database entries into formalized monthly and quarterly reports suitable for DOH submission. These generated PDF files are assigned unique identifiers and stored securely within the system for future retrieval and audit, ensuring accuracy and compliance with official health standards.
